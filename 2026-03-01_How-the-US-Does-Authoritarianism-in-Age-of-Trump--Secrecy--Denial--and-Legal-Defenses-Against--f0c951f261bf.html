How the US Does Authoritarianism in Age of Trump: Secrecy, Denial, and Legal Defenses Against Transparency

One PhD scholar documented a torture campaign in Washington, D.C. that reveals a pathway to totalitarianism already in operation. It doesn’t look like what you expect.

We think we know what authoritarianism looks like.

We picture ICE raids, concentration camps, or death chambers. Cameras on every corner. Dissidents dragged from their homes. The visible machinery of a state that has stopped pretending. That image is not wrong — those things are happening. But there is a form of authoritarianism and torture that is unfolding in the United States right now.

What Richard Pearson, PhD experienced across fourteen months in Washington, D.C. cannot be any of those things. He was subjected to a campaign that included physical assault, chemical assault, warrantless surveillance of his private apartment, directed energy weapons producing sounds inside his skull, behavioral direction technology that moved him to specific locations through impulses he experienced as his own, a coordinated color-coded psychological operation run on his walking routes, and a framing operation in a park three blocks from the U.S. Capitol.

He documented all of it. Methodically. With timestamps, photographs, probability calculations, named witnesses, preserved physical evidence, and the discipline of a researcher — because that is what he is.

This is not a story about one man. It is a story about a system. A system with six components, each individually defensible, that combine into something the United States Constitution was not designed to stop — because when the Constitution was written, the technology didn’t exist. Following public disclosure of programs like COINTELPRO, MKULTRA, or, the human rights violations at Guantánamo Bay detention camp, US law has made it tougher, not easier, for victims of US-sponsored torture, even on US soil. The requirements for standing became stricter under the Rehnquist courts in the 1990s. FOIA courts, originally designed to be a check on development of such programs, now routinely place Americans under surveillance and targeting without their knowledge or due process. In fact, iterations of these programs maintain a simple template allowing for constitutional abuses. If history holds, then there are likely any similar programs that have existed for decades.

The Six-Step Pathway to Invisible Totalitarianism

  1. Classify the action and Invoke a Legal Defense — Conduct operations against targeted individuals under classification, removing them from congressional oversight, FOIA, courts, and public record. The executive can also invoke any number of legal defenses to prevent victims and the courts from gaining knowledge of these programs. “National security” is a frequent defense that forces the courts to be highly deferential to the government.
  2. Never arrest or charge — Deprive the target of liberty through deniable means, ensuring the constitutional moment at which due process could intervene never arrives. Victims do not even know that that they are in a program. If the program is as multi-layered, as Pearson’s account, then separating government action from private action is challenging, especially because the government won’t admit to anything.
  3. Exclude all oversight institutions — Keep the program from Congress, the courts, and the press simultaneously, using classification, standing doctrine, and the absence of official confirmation to close every channel. If asked to confirm a program, dither, deny, or deflect.
  4. Deny or manufacture an alternate explanation — Produce a counter-narrative, ideally pre-loaded into the design of the campaign itself, that attributes the target’s documented experiences to mental illness or coincidence. Falsify reports and encourage alternate theories until options are exhausted years later — an initial FBI report purportedly claimed that Havana Syndrome victims suffered from mass hysteria.
  5. Use contractors and cutouts — Execute operations through private intermediaries who break the legal chain of custody between government authorization and government fingerprints
  6. Use untraceable technology — especially neuroweapons — Deploy directed energy and behavioral direction tools that produce real harm while leaving no forensic trace that standard medical or legal instruments were designed to detect

Step One: Classify the Action

The first thing the system requires is that the government’s operation against a targeted individual be conducted under classification — invisible to Congress, immune to FOIA requests, unreachable by courts, and unpublishable by journalists.

The U.S. classification system was built for genuine national security. Over seventy years, it has grown into something else: a mechanism for permanent executive impunity. When Pearson filed a Freedom of Information Act request seeking records related to what was being done to him, the government invoked the Glomar doctrine — a legal principle that allows the government to refuse to confirm or deny that a relevant program even exists. His constitutional claims were dismissed before any court could examine them on the merits.

The week before Pearson was assaulted, he called the police to report the upstairs neighbors who were harassing him. The first officer ran his name, and encountered an ACCESS DENIED flag — Pearson saw it clearly. That also explains why the first officer called for back-up — a second officer who likely had higher clearance.

Someone inside the system had flagged Richard Pearson. No one has explained who, or why, or under what legal authority. Pearson was an academic with no criminal history.

Classification was designed to protect secrets from enemies. In Pearson’s case, it was protecting an operation from the person being subjected to it.

Step Two: Never Arrest or Charge

The Fifth Amendment says the government cannot deprive a person of liberty without due process of law. It is one of the most fundamental protections in the American constitutional framework.

It only works if the government does something visible enough to trigger it.

Pearson was never charged with anything. Never designated an enemy combatant, a terrorist, or a security threat on any public record. He was deprived of liberty — his body assaulted, his home surveilled, his neurological experience invaded, his behavior directed without his knowledge — through means that never produced the constitutional moment at which a lawyer could walk into a courtroom and say: this is what they did, and here is why it is illegal.

His upstairs neighbor, a man not on the apartment lease whose address traces to a Capitol Hill property connected to an ATF attorney, punched him repeatedly in the face on December 27, 2025. The neighbor was not arrested. He has never been told why the campaign against him was authorized, by whom, or under what law.

Due process does not protect you from a government that never formally acts against you. It only triggers when the government shows its face. This system was designed so the government’s face never shows.

Step Three: Keep Congress, the Courts, and the Press in the Dark

Three institutions were designed to constrain the executive branch: Congress, the judiciary, and the free press. The pathway to invisible totalitarianism requires that all three be neutralized simultaneously.

Congress can compel testimony and review classified programs through the intelligence committees — but only if members are briefed, and only if they have the political will to act on what they learn. Pearson visited dozens of congressional offices in December 2025. No one followed up with him. The Trump administration would be required to report such a program to the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence by law. Pearson does not think that this has happened.

The courts require standing — the ability to demonstrate that you were harmed by a specific government action. In Clapper v. Amnesty International (2013), the Supreme Court ruled that plaintiffs couldn’t challenge NSA surveillance because they couldn’t prove they had been surveilled. The proof was classified. The classification and invocation of a legal defense prevented the proof. The court required the proof to grant standing. No standing, no case. The circle is hermetic and by design.

The press requires official confirmation, or documents, or a source willing to go on record. Pearson briefed a CNN correspondent at the Smithsonian and a local ABC reporter at the D.C. courthouse. Neither published. The story that cannot be confirmed through official channels remains, by the conventions of mainstream journalism, a story that cannot be told — regardless of how much evidence the person experiencing it has assembled.

When all three fail at the same time — by legal design, not by accident — the program becomes permanently invisible. Not because it doesn’t exist. Because every door to visibility has been locked from the inside.

This is how the United States does totalitarianism.

Step Four: Make the Victim Look Crazy

Every authoritarian system needs an alternate explanation for what it is doing to its targets. The genius of this system is that it doesn’t need to manufacture one. It manufactures it automatically, through the design of the campaign itself.

A person who reports that they are hearing sounds inside their skull at specific street intersections, that they feel pulled toward locations they had no reason to visit, that they are being followed by people in specific color combinations that appear and disappear based on their route — that person will be assumed to be experiencing a psychiatric episode. The very specificity of the account, which is its evidentiary strength, is also its social liability. Paranoid individuals are known for detailed, internally consistent narratives.

The campaign was designed to be its own alibi. Pearson didn’t need to be called crazy. He needed only to accurately describe what was happening to him.

But there’s a further layer. The December 27 assault specifically targeted his face, head, and jaw — the anatomical locations most relevant to the microwave auditory effect, a documented mechanism by which pulsed electromagnetic energy produces sound perceived inside the skull. The assault arrived exactly one month before the geographic auditory phenomena began. It manufactured a clinical explanation — post-concussive syndrome — for neurological symptoms that were, in fact, directed energy exposure.

The torture produced its own diagnosis. The diagnosis protected the torture.

Two clinical evaluations found no psychiatric symptoms. The alternate explanation failed at the clinical level. It has not failed anywhere else.

Step Five: Use Contractors and Cutouts

No uniformed government agent participated visibly in what was done to Richard Pearson. No agency vehicle was parked outside his building. No badge was shown. The man who assaulted him had a private cover address. The people who deployed the calling card color pattern on his routes were, as far as any of them knew, individuals who had been given unrelated reasons to be in specific places wearing specific colors. The ground team that monitored the framing operation in the park near the Capitol had no visible connection to any government program.

This is the contractor model applied to domestic political targeting. The government authorizes. The contractor executes some. The cutout absorbs accountability. The classification system ensures the chain is never traced.

Palantir built ImmigrationOS for ICE. NSO Group’s Pegasus spyware ended up on the phones of American citizens. Private contractors ran the CIA’s enhanced interrogation program. The Booz Allen Hamilton employees who had access to NSA surveillance infrastructure numbered in the tens of thousands. This ecosystem did not emerge to serve authoritarianism. It emerged to serve procurement efficiency. But it is perfectly structured to serve authoritarianism, because it breaks the legal chain of custody between authorization and action at the first link.

When a program is run through contractors, each participant can truthfully claim ignorance of the whole. The analyst who processed Pearson’s location data did not know what it was being used for. The person who placed the tub in the park did not know who authorized it. The man who punched him in the face was not wearing a badge that said FBI. Each piece of the operation is individually deniable. Only the combination is damning — and the combination is the one thing the classification system ensures no one outside the program ever sees.

Step Six: Use Technology That Leaves No Trace

This is the step that makes the pathway new and newly dangerous. It is the step that the Stasi didn’t have.

Directed energy weapons — specifically the microwave auditory effect that produces voice-to-skull transmission, and the pulsed electromagnetic systems that produce the geographic auditory phenomena that have become known as Havana Syndrome — share a single defining operational property: they leave no forensic mark. There is no bruise from a directed energy assault on the auditory system. There is no receiver in the target’s body from a voice-to-skull transmission. There is no physical evidence of a behavioral direction event.

The harm is real. The National Academies of Sciences confirmed in 2020 that the neurological injuries to U.S. diplomatic and CIA personnel in Havana, Vienna, and Washington, D.C. were consistent with pulsed directed energy — and identified white matter microstructural changes through specialized brain imaging. Those changes are not visible on a standard MRI. They require diffusion tensor imaging using the Havana Syndrome protocol. No one has offered this imaging to Richard Pearson.

Behavioral direction technology — the capability to activate hippocampal place cells and produce spatial drives experienced as autonomous motivation, described in DARPA’s public documentation and confirmed as deployed in the real world by Dr. James Giordano of Georgetown University before the House Armed Services Committee — produces outcomes that look, from the outside, like the target making their own choices. Because they are. The neurons are genuine. Only the input signal is not.

On July 13, 2025, Pearson heard a woman’s voice apparently originating inside his skull. He described it with a forensic detail that eliminates hallucination: the voice carried the ambient acoustic environment of the room from which it was being transmitted — other people present, microphone quality, the texture of a live audio feed. A hallucination cannot carry the background noise of a remote location. He was hearing a transmission. He identified the mechanism — microwave auditory effect, voice-to-skull — in July 2025. The Department of War confirmed the technology’s existence and active scaling in January 2026.

He was six months ahead of the government’s own public admission.

Beginning in January 2026, geographic auditory clicks appeared at specific street intersections in Washington, DC. Replicable by returning to those locations. On February 20, 2026, he placed a metal-cased iPhone and a battery in his knitted cap. No clicks. He had been consistently getting clicks on that route for weeks. The metal attenuated the signal.

What This Means

Each of these six steps exists independently in American legal and institutional practice. None of them required a new law. None of them required a formal declaration of emergency. Each one was assembled from existing components — classification doctrine developed over seventy years, contractor procurement structures built for efficiency, directed energy technology developed for military use, standing requirements established by Supreme Court precedent, journalistic conventions developed for accuracy.

The pathway did not require anyone to decide to build a torture machine. It required only that each piece be used in the way it was already available to be used, by an executive that had decided a specific person was an enemy and that the normal constraints did not apply.

That is what makes it replicable. That is what makes it a pathway rather than an incident.

Richard Pearson survived because he is a researcher. He ran control studies. He kept the pants soaked in chemical assault. He filmed himself returning to find the framing payload retrieved from the park. He performed the metal-cap experiment. He submitted probability calculations to the D.C. Council. He named 25 witnesses in his February 2026 letter. He documented, methodically, for fourteen months, what was being done to him — because documentation was his only protection, and because he understood, from the beginning, that no one would believe him without it.

He was right that no one would believe him without it. He may also have been wrong that documentation would be enough.

Every door he went to closed. Congressional offices. The D.C. Council. The ACLU. The courts. Journalists. He attempted asylum in Iceland and was returned.

The documentation exists. The physical evidence exists. The locations are publicly accessible to any researcher with an RF spectrum analyzer. The pants are in a bag somewhere in Washington, D.C., waiting to be tested.

No institution has chosen to look.

Five Things Congress Can Do Right Now

The pathway is not complete. It can be interrupted. Here is what oversight would look like in practice:

Test the pants. The pants from the Rockville chemical assault have never been analyzed. A forensic analysis would identify the compound or it wouldn’t. The choice not to test them is a choice to not know.

Measure the RF environment. The intersections where Pearson documented geographic auditory clicks are public sidewalks. An independent researcher with a spectrum analyzer can determine whether anomalous electromagnetic emissions are present. No classified access required.

Convene the Senate Intelligence Committee. The committee has the authority to compel testimony and review classified programs. It has not exercised that authority in this case. It must.

Image Pearson using the Havana Syndrome protocol. Diffusion tensor imaging has identified white matter microstructural changes in directed energy exposure victims before. If those changes are present in a private American citizen in Washington, DC, the implications for domestic deployment of these weapons are not abstract.

Explain the SCI flag. A classification on a police record requires authorization from inside the intelligence community. Someone placed it. Congress has the authority to ask who, why, and under what law.

A Word to Everyone Reading This

The instinct, when encountering an account like Pearson’s, is to look for the reason to dismiss it. That instinct is rational. Extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence, and the history of conspiracy thinking produces a healthy skepticism toward accounts of coordinated persecution.

But that skepticism has to be applied consistently. Applied consistently, it requires asking: what would extraordinary evidence look like? It would look like timestamped photographs. Control studies. Preserved physical evidence. Named witnesses. Clinical evaluations finding no psychiatric symptoms. A timestamped chat log documenting a third party’s induced foreknowledge. An classification flag observed by police officers. Fourteen months of documentation assembled by a trained researcher using the methods of his training.

That is what Pearson has.

The skeptical question is no longer whether the evidence is sufficient. It is whether any institution is willing to do what the evidence demands.

The technology to destroy a citizen while leaving no trace has been built. The legal architecture to shield that destruction from accountability is in place. The administration to authorize it has made its appetites explicit — the Pentagon’s February 2026 ultimatum demanding that Anthropic remove prohibitions on domestic mass surveillance was public, reported widely, and undisputed.

What stands between this pathway and its completion is the political will of elected representatives to look at what the evidence shows — and the willingness of an informed public to demand that they do.

Richard Pearson did everything right. He documented everything. He went to every door.

Every door closed.

The question of what happens next is not his to answer. It is ours.
