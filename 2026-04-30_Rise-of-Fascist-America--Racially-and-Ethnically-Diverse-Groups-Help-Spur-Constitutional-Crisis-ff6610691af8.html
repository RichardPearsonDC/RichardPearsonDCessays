Rise of Fascist America: Racially and Ethnically Diverse Groups Help Spur Constitutional Crisis

The racially targeted government campaign against Richard Pearson is bad enough — wholesale constitutional and legal rights violations. At least 70. But when diverse individuals began appearing in street theater, harassment, and stalking, Pearson knew that it strongly signaled something more serious — the rise of ‘Fascist America’ has much greater strength than he assumed and poses an existential threat to the US. The only thing that could possibly unite these disparate groups, including government, is lawlessness, anti-constitutionalism, and zero accountability. In other words, united to weaken the US form of government. Self-destruction.

‘Fascist America’ is Racially and Ethnically Diverse

“No one group is inherently good or bad. We need to move beyond that. A lot of people regardless of background are acting out uncontrollably and violently, as if having a severe psychiatric episode. They are getting away with much of it...” — Richard Pearson, PhD

Blacks.

The officers who illegally surveilled Richard Pearson’s home, falsely arrested him in December 2025, and booked him on a false charge were Black and Hispanic.

The officers who dismissed Pearson’s complaint about his neighbor’s noise harassment were Black. One was Officer Biscoe. The prior week to his false arrest, Pearson called the police to report his upstairs neighbors who had been following him since the summer and dropping objects wherever he moved — harassment. The officer’s ignorant response was, “There is nothing illegal about someone following you in your apartment.” He was wrong, of course. The officer knew that Pearson’s apartment was under surveillance and that Pearson was being targeted. Officer Biscoe only arrived on the scene after his partner — also Black — was prevented from bringing up Pearson in the OCTO database, “ACCESS DENIED.” Pearson saw the message.

The upstairs neighbors —a male and female — who tormented Pearson since the summer of 2025 were Black.

Blacks, almost exclusively men, were involved in street theater — like the man shouting from his car (“You do talk like that!”), the man pointing at his boombox from the ground level as Pearson peered over his fifth-floor balcony, the man on his bike (“Oh, there you are!”) who found Pearson on bike trail late at night, the metro train driver who pointed right (as if off) when Pearson boarded the train, or the man with the homophobic vile and disgusting rant as Pearson passed (“Maybe he will slide into some pu**y.”). All Black. All men.

The young African teen stalking a Black man at the Lincoln Memorial.

The coffee store manager who admitted after months of harassing Pearson — brandishing a whip at one point — , “Yeah, I was coming at you” also Black with a multiethnic identity.

The African woman — a total stranger to Pearson — who approached him only to mock Pearson’s financial situation, “How are you going to travel without a visa?” She laughed. Pearson hadn’t spoken a single word to the woman. He was in the Montreal bus terminal with a ticket but no passport, hoping to head back to the US-Canadian border. Eventually, he took a taxi.

Jews.

David Meyerowitz in Old Town Alexandria was Jewish, so was the “Bartleby man” in Rockville and the lady with the “Bug Zapper” sign. The Bartleby man appeared around 6:30am, supposedly looking for directions to an auction house that opened in 2.5 hours. Not only did he have written turn-by-turn directions and a car, but he was supposedly an art collector. Pearson believes the man had an agenda, “Ok, he did buy me breakfast, but I think he also had one of those devices. I have later pinned and chemically assaulted. He quoted, “I would prefer not to” from the character of Melville’s Bartleby, the Scrivener. This Bartleby man was part of some conspiratorial network”. The man who stood defiantly after Pearson was chemically assaulted — also appeared Jewish. Pearson had his picture.

The woman who kept popping her head around to stare at Pearson. Also, Jewish. Pearson recalls, “I got on this bus. First, the bus was already late by an hour. It was nearly 1am. I settled into the first available seat — maybe the 8th or 9th row. This woman kept turning around and staring at me — eyes wide open, intentional, and unsettling. About the third or fourth time, I told her that she needed to mind her own business. That didn’t stop her. When about 30 minutes into the ride, I noticed that every time that I moved in my seat, someone would cough on the bus. I deliberately moved two or three times to test it. I finally coughed myself after I moved. What happened? Someone crushed like sounded like a can. No one coughed. Psycho people, right?” Pearson is specifically talking about the psycho people on the bus, not any general claim.

Whites.

Whites appeared regularly in street theater like the white woman who used a racial stereotype about Black people and watermelon, the red-shirted women whose diminutive dogs barked aggressively when Pearson approached, the man with the “We’re in control” T-shirt.

The white woman disclosing information about Pearson’s home interior with, “The mattress is against the wall, then you know what’s next…” Pearson had recently placed his mattress against the wall, hoping to seek asylum. His first attempt would not occur for several months.

Most often, the red-and-black gangstalking involved whites in and around his neighborhood. Pearson had photos, license plates, and even home addresses.

One really disturbing scene occurred outside of a DC metro. Pearson was seated at a table near Starbucks. A young white boy — probably no more than 16 years of age — had his phone in his hand and attention directed at Pearson. Behind him, a boy — likely the teen’s younger brother by 5 years— issued a command, “No.” What was the “No” for? Pearson had a strong idea and knew that it helped explained his torture in the US. It is the same technology that three senators are seeking to regulate — the MIND Act of 2025.

Hispanic.

The women in Silver Spring who conspired in tazing my penis were Hispanic, so were most of the red-and-black crew near his residence.

The supervising officer for Pearson’s false arrest was Hispanic — Fereira.

The fat, big-eared Sheriff’s Office deputy who laughed when Pearson reported an assault and declined to act — Hispanic.

Indian.

The people stalking me in the cherry blossom grove were Indian. An Indian man, a white woman, and an African teen were also stalking Black men that same day.

Pearson felt warming of foil as he was checking out at a convenience store, which must have been an electric beam from behind the counter. The man behind the counter — Indian.

The Indian man stalking a Black woman at 4th and M St. Or the Indian woman who kept circling Pearson at a grocery store for no apparent reason.

Asian.

The Asian man with the “OFF” T-shirt after Pearson returned home from escaping in a hotel and asked for a vacation. Pearson was just crossing the street to his apartment.

The Asian woman tracking me on 4th St. Pearson lost the photo of her when he lost his phone. Pearson cropped a much larger photo to show an Asian woman, suspiciously disguised, who stared directly at him. There were about a hundred people in the photo. She stood out.

Then, there is the Asian man’s awkward street theater on the metro. Pearson had discussed this in previous essays.

This tells us something that is deeply disturbing about the state of America. America’s diversity is facilitating the rise of fascism. If they are willing to contribute to the torture of an African American scholar — not even a celebrity or public figure — what else are they willing to do? Undermining the US form of government is a start.
