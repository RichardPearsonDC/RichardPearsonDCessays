Constitutional Protections and “Consent”
The concept of “consent” in these amendments is generally interpreted through the principle of consent of the governed, which suggests that government authority is legitimate only when it is derived from the people through representation and the rule of law.

  • Procedural Due Process: This requires the government to follow fair legal procedures — such as providing notice and a hearing — before it can interfere with an individual’s rights.
