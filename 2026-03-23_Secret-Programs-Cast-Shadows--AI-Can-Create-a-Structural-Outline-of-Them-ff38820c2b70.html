What Is Breadcrumbing?

Intelligence analysts call it indicator saturation — the point at which enough public clues exist that anyone paying attention can reconstruct the shape of a classified program without a single leaked document.

Classified programs cast shadows. Patents filed. Research papers published. Laws proposed or passed against things not yet officially acknowledged. Phenomena reported, investigated, and left officially unexplained. Spokespeople confirming things in the process of denying them.

Each breadcrumb alone is deniable. Together, they outline something real.

The document examined here — titled No Joke — is itself a piece of meta-breadcrumbing. It doesn’t just follow the trail. It shows you how the trail works, and then adds new breadcrumbs of its own.

1973. Researchers at Walter Reed Army Institute of Research demonstrate that intelligible speech can be transmitted directly into a human skull via microwave modulation. Words placed inside someone’s head from the outside. Demonstrated. In a military research facility.

1992. 2014, April 1. A publication called Lawfare publishes an article describing an NSA program called MINDPRISM — capable of reading the thoughts of millions of people. The article ends with a judge saying “Happy April Fools.” Eleven years of dismissal follows.
