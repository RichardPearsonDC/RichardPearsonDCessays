Is a Mass Exodus of African Americans from Washington, DC the Next ‘Great Migration’?

When two white millennials referenced the “Great Migration” in conversation, Dr. Richard Pearson’s ears perked.

On August 10, 2025, Richard Pearson was walking along Pennsylvania Avenue near the US Capitol. A young white millennial couple passed him. Their conversation caught his attention.

White male (30s): “So what do you think is going to happen?

White female (30s): “There was the Great Migration.”

Four words, emphasis on was. She was not making a historical observation. She was answering her companion’s question about what is going to happen, and it resembled the Great Migration.

Her coded answer itself was revealing, even if only by inference. What could cause another “Great Migration” of African Americans? The original Great Migration was the mass movement of over six million African Americans from the rural South to cities in the North, Midwest, and West between 1910 and 1970. They fled white oppression, lynchings, state terrorism, and second-class citizenship. Why would that be relevant in 2025? The couple knew but didn’t say.

This essay attempts to find out. It determines what can be inferred from her analogy. Whatever it is, it has to have a racial component and involve a form of mass oppression or injury.

This essay discusses the experiences of Richard Pearson — the man who was listening. He was no ordinary person. He was a PhD scholar whose life has been turned upside down. He was living in a nightmare of torture, manipulation, and two assaults.

Was his experience scaled? Was the US military technology that brought Pearson to locations or put a voice in his head scaled across the Black population in Washington, DC?

“Yes,” implied the white female in August 2025.

  1. Racial — The US government targeted Pearson, an African American PhD. The Trump administration’s animosity toward African Americans played across executive orders, political rhetoric, agency policies, and criminal prosecutions. By employing the Great Migration, she was saying what it was racial in nature.
  2. Structural oppression — Pearson encountered a multi-level set of threats beginning in 2025 — physical assault, chemical assault, psychological and tech-mediated torture, auditory “clicks” similar to the Havana Syndrome, stalking, and harassment. By employing the Great Migration, she was saying that it was at least as structurally and intimately oppressive as Jim Crow and white terrorism in the US South.
  3. Displacement — Pearson attempted to flee to the Europe Union but was sent back. Millions fled the US South. By employing the Great Migration, she was saying that it could trigger a mass exodus.

The Criteria

The Great Migration had a specific structure. Invoking it as a frame is not casual. It implies seven precise conditions.

One: A racial component. The Great Migration was exclusively about Black Americans. Any invocation without Blackness at its center breaks the analogy entirely.

Two: State-backed structural oppression. The Migration was not produced by individual bad actors. It was produced by a system — law enforcement, courts, employers, landlords, government — operating in coordinated, mutually reinforcing ways backed by the full authority of the state.

Three: Displacement — flight from severe, state-inflicted violation. People left because staying meant bodily destruction. Not inconvenience. Not discomfort. The violation of their physical safety, their bodily autonomy, their right to exist in their own bodies without state interference. The flight was rational, urgent, and desperate.

Four: Population-scale magnitude. The Migration affected millions simultaneously. The reference sets a numerical threshold that cannot be met by individual or small-group targeting.

Five: A catalyst — public exposure. The Migration accelerated around specific moments of disclosed atrocity that reached mass public awareness. Not private knowledge. Not rumor. Public. Media. Undeniable.

Six: Permanent demographic transformation. The Migration did not reverse. It permanently restructured the racial geography of the United States.

Seven: Generational and institutional consequence. The Migration’s effects compounded across generations — reshaping cities, politics, culture, and institutions for a century.

Richard Pearson

Several months after January 20, 2025 — the day the Trump administration signed executive orders designating DEI scholarship and racial equity frameworks as hostile ideology — he documented fourteen months of a government campaign against him with the discipline of a trained researcher. Timestamped photographs. Probability calculations. Control studies. Named witnesses. Preserved physical evidence. Controlled experiments.

What he documented:

On July 13, 2025, near the Capitol, he heard a woman’s voice originating inside his skull — not from a direction, not at his ear. The voice carried the ambient acoustic environment of the room transmitting it: other people present, microphone quality, the texture of a live feed. He heard the room the operators were sitting in. A hallucination cannot carry the background noise of a remote location. The U.S. Department of War confirmed directed energy weapons were operational and being scaled in January 2026 — six months after Pearson identified the mechanism from the inside of his own skull.

By February 2026 he was dispossessed except for a mattress and a suitcase. He attempted asylum in Iceland. He was returned under the safe country doctrine — the doctrine that treats the United States as safe for all its citizens, regardless of what specific citizens are experiencing.

His upstairs neighbor — not on the apartment lease, his address connecting to a Capitol Hill property linked to an ATF attorney — punched him in the face on December 27, 2025, targeting his head specifically when Pearson raised his arms to defend himself. The neighbor was not arrested. The week prior, Richard Pearson saw an ACCESS DENIED flag when the police searched his name in their database.

A woman passing him on Pennsylvania Avenue on August 3 said without stopping: “The mattress is against the wall, you know what’s next.” He had moved his mattress to the wall. Alone. In his apartment. With no digital transmission. That sentence required live audio surveillance of his private home.

The Technology

The behavioral direction Pearson documented rests on confirmed neuroscience.

The hippocampus contains place cells: neurons encoding specific locations that fire as the body approaches them, producing spatial drives felt as autonomous motivation. A 2020 study in Cell demonstrated that targeted activation of hippocampal place cells drives subjects to specific locations — experienced as their own movement. A 2024 study in Nature Neuroscience demonstrated non-invasive modulation of the human hippocampal navigation system using external electromagnetic stimulation, producing measurable changes in spatial behavior without implants or surgery.

DARPA’s Neural Engineering System Design program describes writing to neural tissue non-invasively using electromagnetic waves. Dr. James Giordano of Georgetown University, the program’s consulting ethicist, testified before the House Armed Services Committee that tools for writing behavior to the human brain have been loosed into the real world.

Pearson was moved to a specific location through an impulse he experienced as his own. He did not know he was being moved. He found out because he is a trained researcher who had been documenting the campaign for months. Most people would never know.

This is not managed displacement. This is people running from a government that has demonstrated it will use their own minds against them.
