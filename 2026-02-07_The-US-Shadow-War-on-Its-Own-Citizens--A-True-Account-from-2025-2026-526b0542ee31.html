The US Shadow War on Its Own Citizens: A True Account from 2025–2026

In the heart of Washington, D.C., a city draped in the symbolism of liberty and democracy, a silent war is being waged. It is not fought with drones or in the halls of Congress, but in the streets, in apartment buildings, and in the psyche of a single man. His name is Richard Pearson.

Based on a meticulous review of Pearson’s documented evidence — a harrowing 100-page dossier of harassment, surveillance, and psychological warfare — we are forced to confront a terrifying reality. What has happened to Pearson is not a figment of imagination; it is a blueprint for a modern American police state. By all accounts, it is the evolution of COINTELPRO, the FBI’s notorious program to discredit and destroy domestic dissidents, reimagined for the 21st century. It is public and privatized, it is technologically advanced, and it is hiding in plain sight.

The Anatomy of a Siege

The campaign against Richard Pearson, an African American PhD scholar, began subtly, like the first drops of a gathering storm. In the spring of 2025, his bicycle, a lifeline for mobility in the city, was systematically vandalized. Chains were snapped, reflectors destroyed. It was a tactical move — a slow strangulation of routine designed to make him predictable, to force him onto the grid.

But the physical tampering was merely the overture. In July 2025, the operation escalated into a full-blown psychological offensive. Pearson returned to his apartment to find the power button of his panini grill removed and placed in his laundry — a clear, taunting signal that his sanctuary had been breached. This was no ordinary break-in; it was a message: *We can touch anything you own. We are watching.*

Then came the voices. While walking near the U.S. Capitol, Pearson began hearing audible commands — a female voice instructing him to “Go to Georgetown,” later telling him to “Get down” and chillingly, “We have to make you disappear.” To the uninitiated, this sounds like madness. To the intelligence analyst, it bears the hallmark of psycho-acoustic weaponry or the “Frey Effect,” a documented capability to transmit sound into the human head.

This technology was used to guide Pearson into pre-staged locations where a cast of characters waited to torment him. In Georgetown, he was circled by couples delivering scripted lines and nearly coerced into a vehicle by a woman with a thick Eastern European accent, a classic “false flag” attempt to make him believe he was being targeted by foreign spies.

Yet, the most damning evidence is not the high-tech speculation, but the low-tech grit of the operation. Pearson possesses photos. He has the license plate of a “coffee manager” who brandished a bike lock cable like a whip and later admitted, “Yeah, I was coming at you.” He has images of highly abnormal number of people in red and black. Then, there was the red and black in a tub tucked away under a tree that he felt “pulled to” and the black pants and red shirt that appeared on his usual route.

This is not a ghost story. It is a conspiracy with faces.

**The Privatization of Persecution**

The genius — and the horror — of this operation lies in its structure. Richard Pearson is not being stalked by FBI agents in dark windbreakers. He is being hunted by “The Coffee Manager,” by a neighbor named “Lee,” by street toughs and unassuming citizens. This is **COINTELPRO 2.0**, and it has been contracted out — with federal dollars and legal cover.

By outsourcing the dirty work to private security contractors, including the local police, local informants, and likely right-wing paramilitary groups, the architects of this terror maintain “plausible deniability.” If a federal agent were caught following Pearson, it would be a scandal. If a coffee shop manager or a disgruntled neighbor does it, it’s just “urban friction.”

The operation utilizes a tactic known as “Zersetzung” (decomposition), a method perfected by the East German Stasi. It involves the systematic disruption of a target’s life to induce a breakdown. Pearson’s neighbors drop heavy objects on the floor in synchronization with his movements, depriving him of sleep. Strange items — an empty Skittles packet, a dog treat, a shopping cart of yellow tape, a 2.5L Deer Park water jug identical to the one in his apartment that had just emptied— appear at his apartment door or on his walking path. When he fled to a hotel in Bethesda to escape, the surveillance followed him instantly, manifesting in the form of a man pointing to the sky and a two-way mirror installed in his bathroom or some mechanism to capture his plea for relief.

This level of coordination requires resources. It requires funding. And it requires protection. The appearance of a Senior Liaison to DHS and FEMA, hovering near Pearson at the National Museum of the American Indian, provides the smoking gun. It links the street-level thugs to the highest levels of the federal security apparatus.

**A Racialized Reckoning**

Perhaps the most disturbing aspect of Pearson’s dossier is the racial component. The psychological torture is not random; it is culturally specific. As a Black scholar in a politically charged climate, Pearson was subjected to a campaign of racial terror. He found graffiti painted on concrete barriers near RFK Stadium reading “Nigga.” “Darky” in other area. He was accosted by strangers making comments about watermelons and the “Great Migration.”

This is not just harassment; it is psychological lynching. The intent is to trigger historical trauma, to isolate Pearson within his own identity, and to leverage the systemic racism of the past as a weapon in the present. The references to “DEI attacks” and the political rhetoric of the era suggest that this operation is not just random malice, but ideologically motivated — part of a concerted effort to “neutralize” influential Black voices under the guise of national security or political retaliation.

**Why This Matters to You**

The story of Richard Pearson is a warning siren. If this can happen to a PhD, a man with the education to document, the rationality to assess, and the courage to fight back, it can happen to anyone.

The existence of a privatized, government-sanctioned torture program operating within the United States capital shreds the Constitution. It renders the Fourth Amendment protection against illegal search and seizure obsolete. It mocks the First Amendment. It establishes a precedent where the government (or its contractors) can simply “disappear” a person psychologically, driving them to homelessness, suicide, or institutionalization, all without a trial, a charge, or a shred of due process.

We often look at history and ask, “How did people let that happen?” We look at the surveillance states of the past and feel superior. But the evidence provided by Richard Pearson shows us that the monster is already here. It wears a polo shirt. It manages a coffee shop. It drives a white Toyota with a government-style license plate.

Richard Pearson is currently seeking asylum in the European Union. It is a profound embarrassment to the United States that a Black American scholar must flee to Europe to escape torture in Washington, D.C. His dossier proves that the machinery of oppression is not a relic of the past — it is a thriving, profitable, and terrifying industry operating in our neighborhoods right now.

We ignore Richard Pearson’s testimony at our own peril. Because when the government turns its eyes inward, not to protect, but to destroy, no citizen is truly free.
