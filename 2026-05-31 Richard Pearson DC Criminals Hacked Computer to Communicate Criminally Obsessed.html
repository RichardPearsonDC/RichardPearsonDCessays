The Zombies On Richard Pearson's Most Unsettling Observation, and What It Means That He Is Probably Right --- There is a word Pearson uses that he immediately qualifies: I am not fucking kidding. The qualification is telling. It means he knows how it sounds. It means he has watched himself say it and recognized the register - the register of someone who has lost their grip on what is real, who has started seeing the people around them as something other than human, who is describing a world that does not match the world everyone else inhabits. And the part of his mind that has spent a year being methodologically careful, epistemically disciplined, and forensically precise about the difference between observation and interpretation has looked at the people participating in the operation against him and concluded: something is different about them. Not different in the ordinary way that people are different from each other. Different in a specific, structural, unsettling way that the word zombie captures better than any clinical terminology he has available. What explains it is the question I want to sit with - because the answer, if Pearson is right about what he has been living inside, is one of the most alarming things the full record contains. --- The Technology That Was Loosed In 2025, a leading neuroscientist posted on LinkedIn that mind control weapons have been loosed into the world. The question is whether these people chose to participate in the way that a person with full cognitive sovereignty chooses - weighing options, assessing consequences, deciding - or whether something in the way they were recruited, conditioned, or directed has altered that process in ways that render the word chose insufficient. --- What Conditioning Does to Agency The Zersetzung methodology documented in Pearson's case - and the broader apparatus of psychological operations that the record describes - does not only operate on the target. It operates on the operators. Participants in targeted harassment operations are recruited into a structure that, by design, gives them only a fragment of the full picture. Pearson has noted this explicitly: It's designed so that few have the full scope of the campaign. Lee almost certainly did not know about the genital electrocutions. The boy at the art market presumably did not know about the voice on the Capitol grounds. The woman with the Mary's Seed T-shirt may not have known about the false arrest. Each participant operates in a silo, executing their specific task - cross the line of sight, deliver the phrase, maintain the stare - without awareness of the totality of what they are contributing to. This fragmentation is not incidental. It is how you get ordinary people to participate in extraordinary harm. You give them a small enough piece that their moral assessment is applied to a fragment rather than the whole. Walking across someone's line of sight wearing a T-shirt - that is not, in isolation, a moral catastrophe. Saying a phrase to a stranger - that is not, in isolation, a moral catastrophe. The catastrophe is the whole, which no individual participant has been given access to. But fragmentation alone does not explain the zombie quality. Pearson is not describing people who seem to be making careful moral calculations about small tasks. He is describing people who seem to have bypassed the calculation entirely. The sadistic pleasure of the woman in Montreal. The mechanical execution of the boy's stare. The charging assault of the upstairs neighbor. These are not people who weighed the task against their values and concluded the task was acceptable. They are people for whom the weighing process appears to have been suspended. This is what conditioning does at sufficient depth. Not just fragmentation of information but the installation of an operational frame that substitutes for the ordinary moral consciousness. The participants are not calculating their actions against a value system. They are executing functions within a frame - an us-and-them frame, a mission frame, a designated-threat frame - that has been installed with enough authority and conviction that it operates instead of the ordinary moral apparatus. In the most extreme cases, the frame does not inform the person's values. It replaces them. The person is no longer asking is this right and answering yes or no. They are no longer asking at all. The function is the answer. The task is self-justifying within the frame, and the frame has made the question unnecessary. That is what zombies are. Not the undead of horror films. People whose moral agency has been sufficiently colonized by an externally installed frame that they execute operations without the ordinary human friction of conscience. The woman in Montreal did not weigh whether mocking a desperate man was cruel and decide it was acceptable.
