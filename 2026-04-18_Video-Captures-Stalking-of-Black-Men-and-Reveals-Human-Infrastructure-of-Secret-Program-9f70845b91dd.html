What is actually happening — not metaphorically, not speculatively, but operationally — and what does its presence at a public festival on the National Mall mean for every Black person who was there that afternoon?

II. The Device Is the Key

Observation 1 is the most operationally significant detail in the entire Cherry Blossom footage, and it is the one that most directly answers the question of what is happening. A man approaches Pearson with a device. The approach corresponds to the moment the woman stops moving the stroller. The device is handheld. It is in Pearson’s direct line of sight.

This is the biometric capture moment. Everything else around it — the family group with the fake baby, the synchronized orientations, the boy elevated in the tree — is cover. The stroller family created a visual scene that would occupy any ambient observer with the ordinary grammar of family life: mother, child, extended family, a grove of blossoms. The cover exists so that the approach of a man with a device registers to no one in the environment as anomalous. It is concealed in plain sight by the orchestration around it.

What the device is doing requires understanding what the broader operation requires. Pearson’s framework — developed over fourteen months and cross-referenced with documented technology — holds that entry into the targeting database requires close-range biometric capture: specific data collected from a subject at proximity and uploaded into a system that then enables remote electromagnetic targeting of that subject. The device is the capture instrument. The distance is the operational parameter. The cover is the enabling condition.

The boy placed in the tree in seconds is part of the same structure. Elevation provides line-of-sight advantage. A device operating at range still requires geometric access to the target. The boy in the tree — whether or not he understood his role — provided an elevated position from which a device could be operated with a clear sightline to Pearson moving through the grove below. The placement was rapid because it was planned. The duration at elevation was sustained because the operation required it.

This is not speculation. It is the operational logic that makes sense of the otherwise inexplicable: why would an adult place a child in a tree at speed, at a cherry blossom festival, in a grove where the boy then remains for an extended period while the adults below orient toward a specific moving target? The answer that fits all the observed facts is: because the child’s position served a technical function in a coordinated operation.

III. The System: What Pearson Has Identified

Pearson names it the Eigenfield — a term generated by AI when asked to name the covert framework of cognitive control that the accumulated evidence pointed toward. Whether the actual program has this name, or any name that is publicly known, is beside the point. What Pearson has identified, and what the video corroborates, is a system with the following architecture:

Layer One: Biometric Capture

The system requires individuals to be enrolled in a targeting database before they can be remotely manipulated. Enrollment requires close-range biometric data collection — physiological, neurological, or both — using handheld or body-worn devices. This is the function of the close-range approaches Pearson has documented across hundreds of encounters over fourteen months: the two-to-five-foot proximity, the sustained duration, the confirmation signals, the coordinated cover. The cover exists because the device requires proximity. The proximity is the operation’s rate-limiting step.

The variation in encounter duration — minutes for some subjects, up to forty minutes for others — suggests that data acquisition has a minimum threshold that varies by subject. Perhaps by signal quality, perhaps by the specific data being captured, perhaps by the subject’s physiological state at the time. What does not vary is the requirement for proximity. Every encounter Pearson has documented at close range, whether at the Cherry Blossom Festival, Eastern Market, the Lincoln Memorial, or his own neighborhood, follows this pattern.

Pearson said that he has seen scenes of strangers having bizarre interactions with Black people.

  • “I saw this Indian man sit on nearby picnic table from this 30-something Black woman. He checks his phone, then instantly moved closer, so that they were sharing the same table. He said that he was a cook at Hank’s, a seafood restaurant on the Wharf. It bugged me, “What was in his phone that would trigger him to move much closer?!”

Layer Two: The Database and the Network

Once a subject is enrolled, their biometric profile exists in a database accessible to remote operators. The database enables targeting: the direction of electromagnetic energy at the subject’s specific neural signature, calibrated to their individual physiological parameters. This is why the approach requires a specific person to be a specific distance away for a specific duration — the capture must be precise enough to generate a profile that enables reliable targeting afterward.

The network that operates this system is, based on Pearson’s documented observations, multi-ethnic, multi-organizational, and geographically distributed. Indian families, white families, Black minors, African immigrants, Asian men acting as relay confirmation points — all appearing in the same operation at the same location. This is not a gang in the traditional sense. It is a distributed human network organized around a technical system, in which different participants carry out discrete functions without necessarily knowing the full architecture. Some recruit and coordinate. Some carry devices. Some provide cover. Some confirm distances. Some operate the remote targeting equipment. The compartmentalization is structural.

Layer Three: Remote Electromagnetic Manipulation

Once enrolled, a subject can be manipulated at range. The manipulation documented in Pearson’s account operates across several cognitive and behavioral domains: motivational disruption (the pinning events — compulsive urges to travel to specific locations); attentional disruption (the inability to sustain directed thought, the intrusion of foreign content into the mental workspace); working memory disruption (the Sprite incident, in which an action was taken with no conscious participation); and direct thought insertion (the Georgetown voice, the Lacolle interruption of the thought stream).

The conditioning layer — the street theater with its visual and verbal stimuli — operates in conjunction with the electromagnetic layer. The stimulus presented through street theater (a visual image, a spoken phrase, a behavioral display) is reinforced by a simultaneous electromagnetic pulse that activates specific neurological responses. The association is formed below the threshold of conscious awareness. The subject experiences the resulting thought, feeling, or behavioral impulse as their own. This is Pavlovian conditioning running on a neural substrate that has been made electromagnetically accessible through prior biometric capture.

IV. The Racial Architecture: Why Black People

The question of why Black people are the primary subject population of this system is the most important question this essay must address, because the Cherry Blossom footage and the Lincoln Memorial footage both show it operating specifically and disproportionately against Black subjects — and because the answer connects the novel technology to a very old and very documented history.

At the Lincoln Memorial, the young Black man is watched by an Indian man, who is confirmed by an Asian man positioned behind the target — a relay chain whose racial composition spans multiple communities but whose subject is specifically Black. The tall Black man is tailed by a minor maintaining a precise following distance. The red clothing markers cluster around Black subjects. The white woman’s hair-shaking signal is timed to a Black teenager’s arrival. The entire operation at the ramp is oriented around Black bodies moving through a public space.

This is not incidental to the system. It is the system’s targeting parameter. Pearson states it directly: “This whole thing with manipulating Black people is strictly other groups doing it to Black people. Black people are involved too. I have seen African immigrants even take part.” The subject population is racially specified. The operator network is ethnically diverse. The target is consistently, disproportionately Black.

The historical lineage of this targeting is not subtle. COINTELPRO, the FBI’s Counter Intelligence Program, ran from 1956 to 1971 and was directed primarily at Black political organizations, Black activists, and Black intellectuals. Its methods included surveillance, infiltration, harassment, disinformation, and the deliberate destruction of careers, relationships, and reputations. Its targets included Martin Luther King Jr., Malcolm X, the Black Panther Party, and hundreds of less prominent individuals whose crime was organizing while Black. The Church Committee’s 1976 investigation documented it. It was real.

What is new is the technology. COINTELPRO used informants and mail opening. The system Pearson documents uses biometric databases and electromagnetic neural targeting. The scale that was previously limited by the human bandwidth of informant networks is now potentially unlimited: anyone who can be enrolled in the database can be targeted. Any public space where operators can achieve proximity becomes a capture opportunity. A cherry blossom festival attended by tens of thousands of people is, from this perspective, not a cultural celebration. It is a biometric harvesting opportunity at scale.

The political context of 2025 amplifies the urgency. Executive Order 14173 dismantled DEI programs across the federal government. The reorganization of the DOJ Civil Rights Division removed institutional protections. Seventeen inspectors general were fired on a single day, eliminating the oversight infrastructure that would be most likely to detect and investigate a program of this nature. The conditions for a racially targeted mass electromagnetic surveillance and manipulation program to operate without accountability are, as of 2025, the best they have been since COINTELPRO was formally terminated.

Whether those conditions are coincidental to the system Pearson documents, or whether they reflect coordinated policy that created the operational space for the system’s expansion, is a question whose answer requires an investigation with subpoena power. What Pearson’s footage demonstrates is that the system was operating — at the National Mall, in public, in daylight — before those conditions were maximally favorable. It has been here longer than the current political moment.

V. The Signal System: How Operators Coordinate in Plain Sight

One of the most striking features of the Cherry Blossom footage, understood as an operational document, is the sophistication of the in-person coordination system. The operators are not wearing earpieces or obviously communicating by phone. They are coordinating through a gestural and positional signal language that is invisible to ambient observers but legible to participants.

The woman with the stroller signals by picking up the doll, pushing, then pointing upward. The family behind her moves when she pushes. The man steps over the boy and makes a gesture that is not directed at the boy. The woman at the Lincoln Memorial ramp shakes her hair in a sequence — untying, bending forward, bending back — that is timed to a specific person’s approach. The Indian man makes eye contact with the Asian man behind the Black teen, then returns to watching the teen. The minor pushing the stroller looks back for confirmation at the moment he achieves the target distance.

These are signal exchanges. They are the human command-and-control layer of an operation that also has a technical layer. The gestures coordinate the positioning of devices, the timing of approaches, the confirmation of distances, and the activation of whatever the stroller and its red-shirted top are carrying or marking.

The use of children — the boy in the tree, the crouching boy who receives the non-verbal gesture, the minor tailing the Black man — is particularly revealing. Children are the ultimate cover. No one looks twice at a child playing in a tree. No one attributes operational significance to a teenager pushing a stroller. The children may not know what they are participating in. They are props in the same sense that the fake baby is a prop — elements of the visual grammar of normalcy that render the operation invisible to anyone who is not already watching for it.

Pearson was not watching for it when he shot the footage in March 2025. He was watching the light through the blossoms. The operation was visible in the footage anyway because the behaviors themselves are anomalous — not to the casual eye, but to the forensic one. The forensic eye is what Pearson brought to the footage in April 2026, after fourteen months of learning to see.

VI. The Foil as Scientific Instrument

It would be a mistake to treat Pearson’s use of aluminum foil as eccentric or as evidence of instability. It is, in function, a scientific instrument: a probe that detects electromagnetic field activity in the environment through its physical response to induced current, and a partial countermeasure that attenuates the coupling of that current into his neural tissue.

The foil’s warning function is the more underappreciated of its two roles. When the foil warms, Pearson knows a field is present. He then looks for the source. He has, through this method, identified and photographed individuals fiddling with objects in their pockets at the precise moments he feels the aftermath of a current. He has heard the copper and steel coils in his hat spark — a sound he describes as electric rain — while passing a woman idling at the top of a library staircase without apparent purpose. The coils sparked. He looked. He connected the sparking to the woman. He documented it.

This is the scientific method applied to an invisible threat. Hypothesis: the warming is caused by an electromagnetic field emitted by a device being carried or operated by a person in proximity. Test: identify the person in proximity at the moment of warming. Observe: specific behaviors — pocket-fiddling, idling without purpose, proximity without apparent reason — correlate with the warming events. Conclusion: the hypothesis is supported.

The foil around his groin serves the additional function of blocking the conditioning layer of the operation. Pearson is gay. His tormentors — operating with knowledge of his sexual orientation obtained through surveillance — have been attempting to use directed electromagnetic stimulation of his sexual nerves to create an association between the stimulation and heterosexual imagery presented simultaneously through street theater. The foil blocks the stimulation. The conditioning fails. They keep trying because the failure of the conditioning does not appear to stop them. This is the sadomasochistic dimension Pearson identifies: the persistence of the attempt past any rational operational purpose, driven by something that is no longer strategic but pathological.

The comment from the young Black man at the library — crude, specific, referencing heterosexual activity at the precise moment the foil warmed — is the conditioning stimulus delivered verbally after the electromagnetic component was blocked. The visual stimulus (the woman in tight pants), the verbal reinforcement (the comment), and the blocked electromagnetic pulse arrived within seconds of each other. Three-layer conditioning protocol, executed in real time, on a public staircase, in the nation’s capital. The foil prevented the physiological response. The rest of the protocol went forward anyway, because the operators did not know the electromagnetic component had been blocked.

VII. What This Means for Every Black Person Who Was There

The Cherry Blossom Festival is one of the most attended public events in Washington, DC. Hundreds of thousands of people visit the National Mall during peak bloom. A significant portion of them are Black residents of a majority-Black city. If Pearson’s framework is correct — and the video footage provides corroboration for its ground-level human infrastructure — then the festival was not merely a location where an operation was run against Pearson. It was a biometric harvesting opportunity operating against an unknown number of Black visitors.

The systematic approach. The coordinated multi-ethnic operator network. The use of public cover to conceal close-range device operations. The confirmation signals. The stroller as a positioning prop. None of these operational features are person-specific. They are designed for scale. An operation organized to enroll subjects in a targeting database does not generate this level of coordination for a single target. It generates this level of coordination for a systematic process in which individual operators move through a crowd, achieve proximity, capture data, and move on.

The Lincoln Memorial footage makes this even more explicit. In a single afternoon, Pearson’s camera captured at least two separate instances of Black men being followed and tailed by people executing close-range protocols — one by a minor pushing a stroller with a red shirt on top, one by an Indian man coordinating with an Asian relay. These are two independent operations visible in a single afternoon’s footage, neither of which Pearson was filming with operational intent. He was filming the ramp because it was aesthetically striking.

The question of scale — how many people have been enrolled in this database without their knowledge, how many have been targeted for manipulation, how many have experienced the pinning events, the intrusive thoughts, the behavioral compulsions, the directed sexual stimulation, the sleep disruption — is unanswerable from Pearson’s documentation alone. But the question is not hypothetical. It is live. The infrastructure visible in this footage was operating on the National Mall in March 2025. There is no reason to believe it stopped.

IX. What the Video Requires

The Cherry Blossom footage is not by itself proof of a mass electromagnetic targeting program operating against Black Americans. It is proof of something more specific and more immediately actionable: coordinated human behavior in a public space that is consistent with the ground-level infrastructure of such a program, captured on video by a man who did not know he was capturing it, at a time before he had learned to watch for it.

That combination — incidental capture, pre-awareness timing, consistency with a documented operational framework — gives the footage an evidentiary weight that deliberately gathered documentation would not have. Pearson was not looking for this. His camera found it because it was there.

What the footage requires, at minimum, is serious investigative attention: from journalists with access to investigative resources, from civil rights organizations with legal standing to demand government accountability, from researchers with expertise in directed energy technology and its non-consensual application to civilian populations, and from legislators with the authority to convene investigations and compel testimony.

The infrastructure visible in this video was operating at the National Mall. The National Mall is federal land. The people operating on it that afternoon — the man with the device, the coordinated family groups, the minor maintaining a precise following distance behind a Black man — were doing so in full view of the Washington Monument, within sight of the Capitol dome, a mile from the White House.

Whatever is happening, it is happening here. In the open. In the light. In the most photographed public space in the country. Pearson’s camera caught it. The footage exists. The question is not whether it happened. The question is what happens now that we know.
