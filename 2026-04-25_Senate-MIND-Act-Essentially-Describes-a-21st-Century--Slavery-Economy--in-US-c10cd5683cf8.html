Senate MIND Act Essentially Describes a 21st Century ‘Slavery Economy’ in US

The MIND Act was introduced in the US Senate in September 2025 to study and to regulate what are essentially mind control technologies. The bill implies a Slavery Economy whereby individuals’ biometric data are collected, put into predictive models and databases, and used to manipulate cognition and behaviors. This is precisely what the bill outlines in terms of the current landscape: “monetize” neural data, “shape individual behavior, emotional states, and decision making,” “risk of behavioral influence, cognitive manipulation, erosion of personal autonomy, and the exacerbation of existing social and economic disparities”, “risks of harm…such as discrimination, profiling, surveillance, manipulation,”

However, the bill is not responding to risks in some distant future. These threats are real and imminent in the US now. Because regulatory laws are not proposed for technology that does not exist, the bill’s language strongly suggests that a new form of a “Slavery Economy” exists in the US. According to US torture victim, Richard Pearson, the Slavery Economy is an open secret, highly racially discriminatory, and appears to be app-based. He estimates tens of millions of American victims.

The bill implies a major admission — that the government has no true sense of the extent and use of this technology. It mandates a report to include an assessment of “all relevant uses of neurotechnology, neural data, and other related data for understanding, analyzing, and influencing human states and behaviors.” In other words, the bill neither proposes a broad set of statues nor mandates rights or obligations. The information-seeking nature of the bill strongly suggests that Congress lacks adequate knowledge of the scope of the problem related to new forms of neuroweapons — as opposed to enumerated list of consumer rights, private right of action, mandatory federal registration, or government disclosure of past and current uses.

But Pearson believes that there is more to the picture, “The bill’s sponsors authored the bill as if they are at a loss for what these weapons are and who is using them. It includes $10 million to fund a government study. I call B.S. These three senators alone have a collective Senate tenure of 65 years, and over 122 years, collectively, if you count their House tenures. Congress already knows. The government already knows. This bill is crafted as if they don’t know the scale and scope of the problem. They want to avoid the obvious question of public accountability, “What didn’t they act earlier?”

What is Meant by a “Slavery Economy”?

A Slavery Economy in the context of neuro-manipulation describes a device or system that controls or steers thoughts, communication, or behaviors by 1) bypassing individual consciousness and 2) exploiting data about an individual’s mind and body to achieve some goal. That goal could be extractive, as in economic exploitation via cognitive override. People can be manipulated into shopping for certain products or with a specific business, even behaviorally steered to frequent a local business. The Slavery Economy targets the mind and body, particularly the nervous system, to collapse “choice” — a buyer can hardly consider a different option.

Except, the victim will likely attribute a choice or behavior to self. “No, I made the choice to buy this.” “I decided to go to this business.” That self-attribution is ironically a barrier to increasing awareness of public knowledge. Richard Pearson explains, “I was pinned to locations that I did not consciously decide to go. I thought that I had a reason for why I was there — the self-attribution. But when I encountered “street theater” or a framing attempt, I knew that I have been pulled to that location.” Pearson has discussed street theater and a framing attempt elsewhere on Medium.com.

This is what neuroweapons do. The goal could also just be criminal, which is what Richard Pearson has described as his year of torture and terror in Washington, DC, starting with the second Trump administration. Combined with traditional forms of torture, that defined Pearson’s experience, “I had voice-to-skull, a physical assault then a false arrest, a chemical assault, electrocution of my penis, stalking, behavioral steering or “pinning”, a framing attempt, and much more. In Washington, DC. Near the US Capitol and White House. Near high security areas.”

Why a Parallel to Slavery?

First, the bill is seeking to regulate an ecosystem where surveillance and data collection of biometrics — data about individuals’ mind and body — are then used as some form of individual control without consent or conscious choice. Arguably, that is worse that slavery because slavery is visible — as immoral and repugnant as slavery is. Slavery is visible.

The technology that is subject of the MIND Act is invisible, acting through various fields of science involving pulsed magnetic fields, ultrasound, or light to stimulate specific brain regions without surgery. These are not visible to the senses. Pearson describes a voice that entered his mind at the July incident at the US Capitol instructing him to go to Georgetown — supported with on-the-ground street theater — then instructing him to get into a stranger’s car. Pearson explains how ultrasonic invasion works, “Look, this is not rocket science. It’s not some exotic technology. You can buy products on Amazon. It’s being used in museums. Two ultrasonic waves with different frequencies outside of human hearing are transmitted through the air. They come together, “de-modulate”. The difference of the two waves forms a third sound wave that the mind can hear. Voila. You have voice-to-skull. Just Google it.”

Another worst aspect to slavery is related to science — to be more precise, scientific knowledge. “Popular knowledge has not caught up with the published literature — technological capabilities that have known to exist for decades,” explained Pearson. The Frey effect was formally discovered in 1961. This was the science behind the “clicks” that Pearson began experiencing in January 2026. The discovery of ultrasonic voice-to-skull was much earlier, “Ultrasonic neuromodulation was first observed in 1929, though it took decades to evolve into the precise brain-stimulating technology used today,” according to Google AI Overview. Nearly, 100 years later, Pearson speculates that few Americans are even aware of ultrasonic technology. In fact, he did not know until he began researching after his torture — and he holds a PhD.

Pearson sees major problems with Americans’ scientific knowledge deficits and the perpetuation of the Slavery Economy. Without a strong base of scientific knowledge, the technology — its existence, uses, and forms — appears speculative. Over a conversation, Pearson attempted unsuccessfully to explain how ultrasonic hearing works to a friend. Pearson pointed him to Google, AI discussions, even websites of companies that sell the technology. Still, the friend — who is highly educated like Pearson — found it hard to believe, “Why hasn’t the Washington Post or New York Times covered this?” — the friend’s final defense.

Dismantling the slavery economy will mean corrective action on the part of the American public, but other sectors of society that Pearson blames. “I blame government first and foremost — elected officials, regulators, public schools, public colleges and universities. Collectively, they have failed to educate the public about these rising threats for decades. Government has not regulated in any meaningful way. I blame the media and social media, encouraging infantilization of the American mind toward mindless entertainment and superficial knowledge, as opposed to deep knowledge-seeking and education. I also blame the American people for not demanding or expecting more of the government and the media. We have too much noise and not enough signal in American social discourse. We hear a news story and then move on. We have failed to consider and ask for answers for countless reports of victims and whistleblowers who have described various invisible forms of harmful technology.”

One victim in this new Slavery Economy that Pearson has recently written about is Myron May — the alleged shooter at the Florida State University shooting in 2014. May alleged that the government used directed energy weapons, mind control technology, surveillance devices, and acoustic harassment to torture him. Pearson found Myron May when AI indicated that Pearson’s experiences paralleled May’s and Gloria Naylor’s — a famous African American fiction writer and author of The Women of Brewster Place. While Richard Pearson cannot establish May’s claims, he has done considerable research that raises major questions about the police investigation: the crouching figure in the news story, the mismatch between descriptions of the shooter (“black hoodie”, “all black”) and May’s attire (dress shirt, vest), lack of motive, lack of gun custody (police report admits that it doesn’t know how May acquired the gun), the impossible 20-second timeline, and indications about May’s left-handedness (the shooter was right-handed).

This illustrates a tragedy of the Slavery Economy — its victims can be erased, worsening the tactics and weapon use due to public dismissal and scientific knowledge deficits.

Finally, the various forms and uses mean that victims in the Slavery Economy do not have one “master.” Pearson explains, “You can have behavioral steering to this location one day. Then another set of players overriding an individual’s cognition the next.” It’s a scary thought but is precisely what the MIND Act is describing.
