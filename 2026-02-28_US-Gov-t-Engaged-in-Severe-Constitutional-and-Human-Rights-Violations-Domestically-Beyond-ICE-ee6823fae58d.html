US Gov’t Engaged in Severe Constitutional and Human Rights Violations Domestically Beyond ICE

Account of a PhD scholar tortured in Washington, DC in 2025–26 finds the United States becoming authoritarian regime unrelated to ICE or immigration. The debate about American authoritarianism has focused almost entirely on immigration enforcement. It should not.

When historians write about the collapse of democratic norms in early twenty-first century America, the ICE raids will feature prominently. The images are available: men in tactical gear, families separated, buses idling outside courthouses. These are visible violations — documented, photographed, litigated, protested. They are real. They are also, in one important sense, a distraction. Because while American attention has been directed toward the border and the detention centers, a different category of state conduct has been unfolding in the interior of the country — against citizens, not immigrants; against a scholar, not a migrant; in the capital city itself — that represents a more fundamental rupture with constitutional governance than anything happening at the southern border.

Immigration enforcement, however brutal, operates within a legal framework that acknowledges its existence. There are courts. There are lawyers. There are journalists at the gate. There are advocacy organizations with standing to sue. The violations are visible enough to be contested.

What Richard Pearson, PhD documented across fourteen months in Washington, D.C. operates in a different register entirely. It is the conduct of a state that has decided one of its citizens can be subjected to a coordinated campaign of psychological torture, physical assault, chemical assault, directed energy weapons, warrantless surveillance, and systematic social destruction — and that no court, no congressional committee, no journalist, and no institution need ever know, because the legal architecture of national security secrecy was designed specifically to ensure that no one ever has to answer for it.

This is authoritarianism without a face. It is more dangerous than authoritarianism with one.

The question of whether U.S. intelligence is involved is not an open question that requires agnosticism. It is a question to which the evidence points in one direction with a force that makes the alternative hypothesis untenable. The US government is the linchpin — directly involved in severe constitutional and human rights violations of a US citizen.

Who Richard Pearson Is

Before the evidence can be evaluated, the evaluator must be established.

Richard Pearson is an African American scholar with a doctorate, whose dissertation is published and publicly accessible. He is not a political operative. He is not a former intelligence employee. He is not a public figure with enemies. He is an academic whose work analyzes systemic racism in research, who lived in a Washington apartment building, in one of the most densely instrumented neighborhoods in the United States, within walking distance of the Capitol, the White House, and the operational center of American national security infrastructure.

On January 20, 2025 — the first day of the new administration — executive orders were signed designating the academic field Pearson inhabits as actionable hostile ideology. DEI scholarship, racial equity frameworks, and liberation philosophy analysis were each targeted for elimination from federal institutions. The administration’s rhetoric in those first weeks used the language of “enemies within,” invoked “urban military training” in the context of domestic operations, and explicitly named the ideological territory Pearson’s work occupies as the enemy’s territory.

His documented harassment began that same week.

He is not delusional. He was evaluated by D.C. Department of Behavioral Health clinicians on September 15, 2025. His mind intact to ask for permission to record the evaluation on his own device. They found no psychotic symptoms requiring intervention. He has since seen three physicians and one social worker. None identified psychiatric symptoms. A mental health professional who reviewed his account said, in his words: “This is some crazy stuff. Sounds like it.” That is not a clinical dismissal. It is a human being encountering a coherent account of something extraordinary and responding with the only honest reaction available: belief.

He documented everything. Timestamped photographs. Probability calculations. Control studies. Named witnesses — 25 identified in his February 2026 letter to the D.C. Council, ranging from Starbucks staff at Federal Center Metro who saw him regularly through his evaluation. Preserved physical evidence. A chat log. Videos. Receipts. The methodology is that of a public health researcher, because that is what he is.

With that established, here is what was done to him.

The Campaign: What the Evidence Shows

Physical Assault and Bodily Harm

The most direct violation is also the most documented.

On December 27, 2025, Pearson’s upstairs neighbor ran down the hallway and repeatedly punched him in the face. Pearson crouched and shielded himself. The assailant went under his arms specifically to reach his face and head when the shield blocked direct contact. Medical documentation confirmed lacerations, a black eye, and bruising to his wrists. He photographed the injuries. He uploaded the photographs to YouTube. He woke the next morning to a face more swollen than the night before.

The man was not on the apartment lease. His given address was linked to a property on Capitol Hill connected to an ATF attorney. He was not arrested. Pearson encountered an ACCESS DENIED flag indicating his file was classified. The office observed it and called for back-up — presumably someone with higher clearance.

A man not on a lease, with a cover address linked to federal legal infrastructure, punches a tenant repeatedly in the face and head — targeting those specific anatomical locations — and faces no legal consequence. The police who respond find a SCI-level classification flag on the victim’s file. This combination does not describe a domestic disturbance. It describes an authorized operation. By Pearson’s account, the assailant had immunity.

In September 2025, at the Rockville Metro station, Pearson’s pants were soaked with an unidentified substance while he sat on the grass. He described it with forensic precision: highly odorous, with properties of industrial adhesive — skin adhesion requiring near-dermis-level force to remove, resistant to soaking, persistent. He preserved the pants. They have never been tested. A D.C. public figure witnessed the aftermath.

These are not incidents that require exotic interpretation. They are physical assaults, documented with medical evidence and preserved physical evidence, against which no legal remedy has been available because the legal system closed before the merits could be reached.

Warrantless Surveillance

The evidence of sustained, intimate warrantless surveillance of Pearson’s private apartment runs through the entire document. On August 3, 2025, a woman in a red shirt passing him on Pennsylvania Avenue said as she passed: “The mattress is against the wall, you know what’s next.” He had moved his mattress to the wall. He had been alone in his apartment when he did it.

This is not the only instance. A man identified as “Lee” — assessed by Pearson as intelligence personnel, described as “conscience-stricken” — demonstrated knowledge of Pearson’s private apartment self-talk when he commented on it in August 2025: “random,” about something Pearson had said alone in his apartment; “not you,” about his dissertation. Knowledge of private speech that was never transmitted digitally or in the hearing of anyone else requires either physical surveillance of the apartment space or audio transmission from within it.

The apartment above his own produced location-responsive noise throughout the summer and fall: objects dropped at the precise moment he moved from room to room, regardless of which room. He moved his mattress to his closet. The noise followed. He moved it again. It followed again. Whatever was happening above him was not a restless neighbor. It was location-aware. It tracked him from space to space within his own home.

Under the Fourth Amendment to the United States Constitution, this is an unreasonable search. The absence of a warrant does not make it legal. The classification of the program that authorized it does not make it legal. The use of a contractor rather than a federal agent does not make it legal. It is a sustained warrantless search of a private residence, conducted against a citizen who has committed no crime, charged with no offense, and against whom no probable cause has been established in any court.

Directed Energy Weapons and Neuroweapon Deployment

On July 13, 2025, at the Capitol, Pearson heard a voice. He described it with the specificity of someone reporting a sensory event rather than inventing one: “The voice sounded like a white woman. And I hear others wherever she was at like she was at a microphone and others were around. There’s a whole bunch of other noise. It almost sounds like there are other people in the room.”

He heard the ambient acoustic environment of the room from which the transmission was originating — the background noise, the microphone quality, the presence of others. This is not the profile of auditory hallucination. Hallucinations are generated by the auditory cortex, which has no access to the acoustic environment of a remote location. This is the profile of a live audio feed transmitted through parametric directional speaker technology — the same technology, in its commercial form, used in museums to project targeted audio to individual visitors that only they can hear.

The Department of War confirmed in January 2026 that directed energy weapons are in active service and being scaled. A Washington Post investigation published February 19, 2026, traced the lineage from Havana Syndrome through the Venezuela operation — Venezuelan security guards described feeling their heads exploding from inside, experiences the Post explicitly noted were nearly identical to the first known Havana Syndrome case. The National Academies of Sciences, Engineering and Medicine concluded in 2020 that pulsed directed energy — specifically the microwave auditory effect, or Frey effect — was the most plausible mechanism for the neurological injuries sustained by U.S. diplomatic and intelligence personnel in Havana, Vienna, and Washington, D.C. itself.

Pearson identified this mechanism by name in 2025, before the government confirmed it in January 2026. He was not reading the news and constructing a narrative. He identified the weapon from the inside of his own experience, and the government confirmed the weapon’s existence six months later.

Beginning in January 2026, one month after the assault that targeted his face and head, Pearson began documenting geographic auditory clicks: sharp, crisp, localized to specific street intersections in Washington, replicable by returning to those intersections. He photographed his precise location at each onset. The National Academies identified this geographic directional specificity — onset at specific locations, cessation on departure, resumption on return — as the diagnostic fingerprint of Havana Syndrome. It is the fingerprint of a focused pulsed energy beam. It does not appear in the symptom profile of any neurological disorder.

On February 20, 2026, Pearson placed a V9 battery and a metal-cased iPhone in his knitted cap before walking his usual route. No clicks. He had been consistently getting clicks on that route for weeks. The metal attenuated the signal.

A man whose apartment had just been used to assault his face and head was subsequently experiencing geographic auditory phenomena consistent with the Frey effect at intersections in the most heavily instrumented national security geography in the country. The assault may have lowered his neurological threshold for detection. Or it may have been designed to produce exactly the alibi that would make his subsequent neurological symptoms attributable to post-concussive syndrome rather than directed energy exposure. Either interpretation is consistent with the evidence. Neither is consistent with the hypothesis that nothing was done to him.

Behavioral Direction and Environmental Control

The most conceptually challenging element of Pearson’s documentation — and the one most important to understand, because it represents the most sophisticated form of the abuse — is the systematic manipulation of his environment and behavior through technology that leaves no mark because it operates through the target’s own experience of reality.

Pearson documented what he called pinning: the experience of being drawn to specific locations through impulses that felt autonomous but terminated at destinations that had been prepared. Georgetown on July 13, where a behavioral compliance assessment appears to have been conducted through the voice-to-skull technology. Adams Morgan on July 17, where he wandered from Chinatown to find a staged encounter waiting. A park on 2nd Street SW on November 7, where a specific directional pull redirected him from the sidewalk, mid-departure, to a precise sub-location under a tree where a plastic storage tub had been placed — containing a red hat, a black shirt, a notebook of conspiratorial writings designed as a framing payload, and two identical photographs of a blond Eastern European-looking man.

He photographed the tub without removing anything. He returned on November 13 with video. The notebook and photographs were gone. The red hat and black shirt remained. The framing payload had been selectively recovered when it failed. Someone knew he had visited, knew he had not taken the materials, and returned to retrieve them while leaving the calling card colors in place. This is a monitored, responsive ground operation with a specific objective, a contingency protocol, and an authorial signature.

The calling card was the color pattern: bright, solid red paired with solid black, deployed on Pearson’s regular walking routes in densities he subjected to controlled statistical analysis. Baseline prevalence across neutral environments — unannounced return visits to Eastern Market, the MLK Library, the Metro underground, a holiday market, neighborhoods he visited without pattern — was approximately 4%. On his regular routes, at the moments of his arrivals and departures, the density was impossible to explain by chance. On September 30, 2025, eleven people in the specific combination appeared within 8.5 minutes in light foot traffic near Eastern Market. His probability calculation: explaining this by chance would require a minimum of 200 people to have been present in an area that was photographically nearly empty.

Confirmation bias does not produce geographically bounded, temporally responsive findings that are absent from unpredictable visits to the same locations. That is the pattern of coordinated deployment, not the pattern of a mind seeking its own projections.

The technology enabling this — real-time location tracking through mobile devices, GPS manipulation, directed electromagnetic influence on hippocampal circuits producing spatial drives experienced as autonomous motivation — is documented in public military neuroscience literature. DARPA’s Neural Engineering System Design program describes bidirectional brain access. Dr. James Giordano of Georgetown University has testified before Congress and stated publicly that tools for writing behavior to the human brain have been “loosed into the real world.” These are not classified claims. They are on the public record.

The February 8, 2026 encounter with his friend — four sequential friction events delaying and rerouting the friend to Pearson’s street, culminating in the friend’s documented foreknowledge: “when I turned I knew I am gonna see you there” — is the external confirmation that behavioral direction technology was being applied not only to Pearson but to people around him. The friend did not know he had been moved. He knew only that he knew.

Psychological Torture: The Architecture of Destruction

What Pearson documented is not a collection of independent incidents. It is a coordinated campaign whose design is legible once its components are understood together.

The bicycle was progressively vandalized — a snapped reflector, broken chain guard, flat tire, ultimately an irreparable wheel — over several months in early 2025. Each damage was individually attributable to normal urban wear. Together, they forced a man who had cycled across the city for a decade onto his feet: slower, more predictable, more easily tracked and positioned around. The bicycle was not stolen. Theft would have ended Pearson’s use of it in a day. The methodical degradation stretched over months, ensuring maximum disruption while maintaining deniability.

The location-responsive noise above his apartment ran throughout the summer and fall. It followed him from room to room. It was not random. It was aware of him. Its function was not merely to disturb his sleep — though it did. Its function was to make his own home a place of inescapable pressure, to eliminate the one space where a person under surveillance might find relief, and to ensure that his waking hours were spent in the one environment where the surveillance was most intimate.

The calling card pattern — the red and black deployed on his routes in statistically impossible densities — served three simultaneous purposes: demonstrating that his movements were known in advance, creating a psychological environment of invisible threat that only he could see and that everyone else would dismiss as paranoia, and accumulating pressure toward a calculated tipping point. The design of the campaign, as Pearson analyzed it, aimed at what he called “neutralization via psychotic break and social death” — the destruction of his credibility, his relationships, his capacity to function, and ultimately his liberty, through means that would be attributed to mental illness rather than state action.

The assault in December 2025 — specifically targeting his face and head — arrived one month before the geographic auditory phenomena began, providing a forensic alibi for any neurological symptoms that followed. The ACCESS DENIED flag on his police record ensures that the full picture of his file remains inaccessible.

Each element of the campaign was individually deniable. The combination was not. The combination is the signature of what the East German Stasi called Zersetzung: the systematic decomposition of a target’s life, relationships, and sense of reality through environmental manipulation, using methods designed to be invisible to outside observers while being inescapable to the target. The Stasi’s operational manuals for Zersetzung are available in the declassified German Federal Archive. The methodology Pearson documented is their contemporary American implementation, updated with technology the Stasi could not have imagined.

The Implausibility of Non-Involvement

The question of whether U.S. intelligence is involved is not an open question that requires agnosticism. It is a question to which the evidence points in one direction with a force that makes the alternative hypothesis untenable.

Consider what non-involvement would require.

It would require that someone other than a U.S. intelligence agency had access to high level classification of Pearson’s police record — the flag that MPD officers encountered and could not penetrate when they ran his name on December 27, 2025. It is not accessible to private actors. It is not a designation that can be placed on a police record without authorization from within the U.S. intelligence community.

It would require that someone other than a U.S. intelligence entity had the operational capacity to deploy directed energy weapons in Washington, D.C. — at the steps of the US Capitol — without detection or intervention by the agencies responsible for monitoring exactly that kind of activity in exactly that neighborhood.

It would require that Pearson’s assailant — a man not on Pearson’s lease, whose given address connects to a Capitol Hill property associated with an ATF attorney — was a private actor conducting a private assault for private reasons, and that his non-arrest by MPD officers was a coincidence rather than a policy.

It would require that the precise timing of the harassment campaign’s onset — the same week that executive orders designated Pearson’s academic field as hostile ideology — was coincidental.

It would require that the campaign’s documented methodology, which tracks with the historical operational signatures of COINTELPRO and the CIA’s CHAOS program with contemporary technological enhancements, was developed independently by private actors without institutional support, technical infrastructure, or legal cover.

Each of these requirements, considered individually, strains credulity. Together, they are not a hypothesis. They are a refusal to look at what the evidence shows.

The more likely explanation — the one requiring the fewest independent assumptions, as Occam’s razor demands — is that an authorized intelligence operation was run against Richard Pearson beginning in January 2025, using directed energy weapons, behavioral direction technology, warrantless surveillance of his private residence, coordinated ground operations, and physical assault by an operative embedded in his building, in retaliation for academic work the administration had designated as hostile ideology.

This explanation requires one assumption. It accounts for every element in the record without remainder. It is the explanation that the entire architecture of Glomar doctrine, national security classification, and FOIA exemptions was designed to prevent from ever reaching a courtroom.

The Constitutional Ledger

The Fourth Amendment prohibits unreasonable searches and seizures without a warrant based on probable cause. The sustained audio surveillance of Pearson’s apartment — evidenced by strangers quoting back his private speech, location-responsive noise tracking his movement from room to room, and a man demonstrating knowledge of apartment self-talk that was never digitally transmitted — is a warrantless search. The classification level of the authorizing program does not make it constitutional.

The First Amendment prohibits government retaliation against speech. Pearson’s dissertation is published academic work analyzing racial equity and liberation philosophy. The executive orders of January 20, 2025 designated exactly this category of scholarship as hostile ideology. The documented onset of the harassment campaign in the same week those orders were signed is government retaliation against protected speech.

The Fifth Amendment prohibits deprivation of liberty without due process of law. A campaign designed — by the evidence’s own internal logic — to produce neutralization through psychotic break and social death is a deprivation of liberty by state action: without charge, without trial, without notice, without hearing, and without any opportunity for the target to contest his designation or confront his accusers.

The prohibition on cruel and unusual punishment embedded in the Eighth Amendment, and the Convention Against Torture to which the United States is a signatory, cover exactly the conduct Pearson documented: sustained, deliberate psychological and physical degradation designed to destroy a person without leaving forensically obvious marks, implemented through deniable means, attributed to mental illness rather than state action. The fact that it is conducted against a citizen rather than a prisoner does not remove it from the framework. The Convention Against Torture contains no such limitation.

The right to bodily integrity, protected by substantive due process under the Fourteenth Amendment, covers being punched in the face by an operative in a government-authorized operation targeting the neurologically sensitive anatomy of the head and jaw. It covers chemical assault at a Metro station. It covers the deployment of directed electromagnetic energy against brain tissue without consent.

Every one of these protections exists in the text of the Constitution and in the international human rights framework the United States has ratified. None of them has been enforced in Pearson’s case. Not because the violations are uncertain. Because the Glomar doctrine, the state secrets privilege, and the classification architecture ensure that no court can reach the question of whether the violations occurred.

Beyond ICE: The Shape of American Authoritarianism

The academic and journalistic literature on democratic backsliding has developed a fairly standard typology: electoral manipulation, press suppression, judicial capture, and the targeting of minorities through visible enforcement. ICE fits this typology. It is visible. It can be photographed, litigated, protested, condemned by international human rights bodies, and contested in courts — however ineffectively.

What Pearson’s case describes is categorically different. It is the use of technology the public does not know exists, authorized through programs that cannot be acknowledged, against a citizen who has committed no crime, implemented in a way that produces symptoms the medical establishment will attribute to mental illness, and insulated from any legal remedy by doctrines that prevent courts from ever asking what happened.

This is not the authoritarianism of jackboots. It is the authoritarianism of plausible deniability, institutional deference, and a legal architecture that was built incrementally across seventy years of national security jurisprudence until it became capable of absorbing the most fundamental constitutional violations without producing a case that any court would hear on its merits.

The historical parallel is not to the obvious authoritarian regimes. It is to the earlier and more insidious form: the East German state that documented its methodology for destroying citizens in operational manuals that became available for public inspection only after the state itself collapsed. The Stasi did not need concentration camps. It needed the capacity to reach into a person’s daily life and make reality itself a source of inescapable pressure, while ensuring that anyone the person told would conclude they were losing their mind. The technology has been updated. The methodology is identical.

There is one difference. Richard Pearson is a trained researcher. He ran control studies. He preserved evidence. He named witnesses. He performed falsification tests. He submitted probability analyses to the D.C. Council. He visited congressional offices. He found mental health professionals whose evaluations are on record. He wrote an account of extraordinary methodological discipline that runs to hundreds of pages.

The Stasi’s methodology relied on the target being unable to document what was being done to them. In Pearson’s case, the documentation exists. The question — the only question that remains — is whether any institution will choose to look at it.

What Should Happen

The pants from the Rockville chemical assault have never been tested. A forensic analysis of the compound would either identify a known agent associated with harassment operations or it would not. The equipment to conduct this analysis exists. The pants exist. No institution has chosen to apply the one to the other.

The locations where Pearson documented geographic auditory clicks are publicly accessible. An RF spectrum analyzer operated by any researcher at 7th Street between Madison and Constitution, E Street NW near the bus stop, or the Southwest Library at 3rd and I would either find anomalous electromagnetic emissions or it would not. No institution has chosen to look.

The chat log documenting his friend’s foreknowledge on February 8, 2026 — four sequential delays and a moment of anticipatory certainty, timestamped, preserved, in the friend’s own words — is available for analysis by anyone with knowledge of GPS manipulation and behavioral direction signatures. No institution has chosen to analyze it.

The classification flag on Pearson’s police record — observed by MPD officers on December 27, 2025 — is either there or it is not. A congressional committee with oversight authority over the intelligence community could ask the question. None has.

The advanced diffusion tensor imaging protocol used to identify white matter microstructural changes in Havana Syndrome cases — changes that do not appear on standard clinical MRI — could be applied to Pearson. The National Academies panel found these changes in diplomatic and intelligence personnel who experienced directed energy exposure. If the same changes are present in a Black equity scholar living in Southwest Washington, D.C., the case for domestic deployment of directed energy weapons against American civilians would move from documented circumstantial evidence to neuroimaging confirmation. No institution has offered him this imaging.

These are not demands for conclusions. They are demands for investigation. They are the minimum that any functioning constitutional democracy owes a citizen who has assembled this quality of documentation of this scale of abuse.

Richard Pearson spent fourteen months building a record that was designed to be impossible to build. He built it anyway. He has been to every door. Every door has been closed — by institutional indifference, resource constraints, Glomar invocations, and the accumulated weight of a legal architecture that was never designed to protect citizens like him from programs like this one.

The conversation about American authoritarianism will continue to focus on ICE until the country is willing to look at what happens when the state turns its most sophisticated instruments not on immigrants who lack standing to sue, but on citizens who have every constitutional right and no practical recourse. Richard Pearson has the rights. He has the documentation. He has the clinical evaluations. He has the physical evidence. He has the methodology.

What he does not have is an institution willing to ask the questions his documentation makes unavoidable.

That absence — the gap between the constitutional promise and the institutional reality — is the shape of American authoritarianism in 2025 and 2026. It is not the shape most people are looking for. That is precisely why it is the shape that will persist.
