The US Government Tortures. It Happens Continuously. On US Soil. Legal Doctrines Make It Easy.

Like Nazi Germany’s denial of the Holocaust, it is time for the US to own up to its forms of torture — funded with taxpayer dollars, using government employees or contractors, and the cover of US law.

The Torture Dungeon: What It Is and Why You Haven’t Heard of It

Most Americans believe their government operates within the law. Courts, civil rights protections, the First Amendment — these are real, and for most people, they work. But alongside that visible system, researchers and survivors describe something else: a parallel apparatus of state-sanctioned harassment, psychological torture, and surveillance targeting specific individuals — intellectuals, activists, whistleblowers, and people of color with public influence — while leaving almost no legal trace. Call it the Torture Dungeon.

This isn’t a building. It’s a condition — a persistent operational environment engineered to be invisible, unaccountable, and self-concealing.

The History That Built It

This didn’t appear from nowhere. It has a documented lineage.

After World War II, Operation Paperclip brought over 1,600 Nazi scientists and physicians into American military and intelligence programs — including researchers who had conducted experiments on concentration camp prisoners. Their expertise in psychological manipulation, behavioral control, and the biological effects of electromagnetic radiation fed directly into classified American programs.

In the 1950s, the CIA launched MKUltra — a program of 150+ secret subprojects at 80 institutions researching mind control, drug-induced amnesia, and behavioral manipulation. One subproject funded psychiatrist Ewen Cameron, who kept patients chemically sedated for months while subjecting them to electroshock at 40 times the normal dose and looping audio messages into unconscious minds. Patients arrived with mild depression and left with destroyed identities. The CIA funded it. Canada eventually paid reparations. The U.S. never did.

CIA Director Richard Helms destroyed most MKUltra records in 1973. What was accidentally preserved and released in 1977 represents only a fragment of a program whose full scope the destruction was designed to erase.

Simultaneously, the FBI ran COINTELPRO from 1956 to 1971, targeting Black civil rights organizations, antiwar groups, and individual writers and intellectuals. Tactics included forged letters designed to incite internal violence, blackmail, fabricated evidence, and coordination with local police. The FBI sent Martin Luther King Jr. an anonymous letter urging him to commit suicide. Fred Hampton, the 21-year-old Black Panther leader, was killed in a pre-dawn raid using a floor plan provided by an FBI informant.

Congress exposed COINTELPRO in 1975 and passed new oversight laws. The underlying apparatus — contractors, informants, surveillance technology — was not dismantled. It evolved.

The Technology That Makes It Possible

In 1961, biophysicist Allan Frey demonstrated that pulsed microwave radiation could create auditory sensations perceived inside the skull — sound that bypasses the ears entirely. By the early 1970s, military researchers had demonstrated intelligible speech transmission through this mechanism. The Army maintained a public entry called “Voice to Skull Device” in its research database until quietly removing it.

This is not science fiction. Multiple patents document the underlying physics. A neuroethicist at Georgetown University who advises the Pentagon stated publicly in 2025 that mind-reading and behavioral influence technologies have been “loosed into the world.” A 1995–1996 U.S. Air Force advisory document explicitly discussed non-lethal weapons designed to produce “a condition of learned helplessness” through neurological targeting.

The technology exists. It has been refined in classified programs for sixty years. The question is how it’s used.

How the Operation Works: The Rooms

Survivors describe a consistent set of tactics — what researcher Richard Pearson calls “rooms” in the Dungeon. They are deployed simultaneously, each designed to be deniable in isolation and devastating in combination.

Sleep Deprivation. Through-wall radar technology can track a person’s location within an apartment in real time. Using this, operatives direct sound — knocking, footsteps, machinery — to follow a target from room to room at 3 AM. Sleep deprivation is internationally recognized as torture. It also produces paranoid ideation, emotional breakdown, and cognitive impairment that looks clinically identical to early psychosis. This is the point.

Environmental Harassment. Chainsaws, construction noise, and car doors slamming in coordinated patterns outside a target’s window. The frequency and precision are perceivable only to the target; to anyone else, it’s ordinary urban noise. Over time, the target becomes conditioned — they begin to perceive threat in ordinary sounds, doing the work of harassment themselves.

Home Entry. Entry without theft. Objects displaced. Attic insulation disturbed. A button on a kitchen appliance repositioned. The message: we were here, we can enter at will, and there is nothing you can record that anyone will believe.

Social Network Infiltration. In author Gloria Naylor’s documented account, a trusted friend of ten years was recruited as an informant under threat — his prior arrest made to disappear in exchange for access to her laptop and home. The operational goal isn’t just information. It’s to destroy the target’s ability to trust anyone, converting their primary support system into a source of threat.

The Voice. The most technically advanced room and the most operationally precise: using microwave auditory technology, a voice is delivered directly into a target’s perception — coming from a specific direction, with background acoustic noise suggesting an operational environment, saying things the target would never say to themselves. Both Naylor and Pearson describe voices that stopped when they moved beyond a certain range, suggesting a localized device rather than internal generation. The psychiatric system, without knowledge of this technology, diagnoses auditory hallucinations. The misdiagnosis is the operation’s intended outcome.

The Legal Architecture That Seals It

The operation is not just protected by secrecy. It is protected by law — by a layered architecture of legal doctrines specifically designed to terminate lawsuits before any court examines the underlying facts.

State Secrets Privilege (established 1953): The government can assert that a case involves state secrets. Courts defer. No discovery, no merits review, case dismissed. When the founding documents were finally declassified, they contained not national security secrets but evidence of government negligence — the very misconduct the privilege had concealed. The doctrine built on that fraud remains in effect.

Sovereign Immunity: The government can be sued for slip-and-fall accidents in federal buildings. It cannot be sued for torture conducted through classified programs. This distinction was not accidental.

The Elimination of Bivens: A 1971 ruling created the right to sue individual federal officers for constitutional violations. The Supreme Court has spent fifty years narrowing it to near-nonexistence. Any technology developed after 1971 is a “new context” — no claim allowed. Voice-to-skull technology: no claim. Directed energy weapons: no claim. The novelty of the method is built-in legal immunity.

Qualified Immunity: Even where a claim theoretically exists, courts require a prior ruling on near-identical facts. Since no case involving these technologies has survived threshold dismissal to reach a merits ruling, there is no prior ruling. Every deployment is immune. The first and the hundredth.

Researcher Pearson documented over one hundred such legal mechanisms, each designed to terminate a claim before the next layer is needed.

The Trap

Every tactic produces a symptom that looks like a psychiatric condition. Sleep deprivation looks like psychosis. Perceived surveillance looks like paranoia. Voices look like schizophrenia. When targets describe what is happening to them, friends and doctors — reasonably, without any malice — apply the closest available diagnosis. Treatment begins. It doesn’t work, because it addresses the wrong cause. The treatment failure is recorded as evidence of severe mental illness. The target’s entire account is resealed under the psychiatric label.

The system doesn’t need to silence these people. It makes them impossible to believe.

What Remains

Both Gloria Naylor — National Book Award-winning novelist — and Richard Pearson — PhD researcher — obtained preemptive mental health evaluations and built comprehensive documented records specifically to defeat this mechanism. Both are Black American intellectuals whose professional training gave them exactly the tools the operation was designed to destroy.

The Dungeon was designed for the person who falls apart. Who stops trusting their own perception. Who can’t maintain systematic documentation under sustained psychological attack.

It was not designed for someone who understands the trap in advance, timestamps every photograph, and puts the record in public before any classification order can reach it.

You know this now. What you do with the knowing is the only question the system didn’t design for.
